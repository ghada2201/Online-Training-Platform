Post-Training Assessment

1. Social engineering attacks primarily target which of the following?

  • a) Network firewalls and antivirus software.
  • b) Human behaviour and decision-making processes.
  • c) Server vulnerabilities.
  • d) Outdated software.

2. What is a common technique used in spear phishing?

  • a) Sending mass emails to random individuals.
  • b) Crafting personalised emails targeting specific individuals or organisations.
  • c) Using automated tools to scan for system vulnerabilities.
  • d) Infecting devices with malware through USB drives.

3. If you receive a call from someone claiming to be from your bank, asking for your account details, what should you do?

  • a) Provide the information if they seem trustworthy.
  • b) Hang up and call the bank using the official number from their website to verify the request.
  • c) Ask the caller to send you an email for confirmation.
  • d) Ignore the call and do nothing.

4. Which of the following is NOT a recommended practice for protecting against social engineering attacks?

  • a) Regularly updating your passwords and using multi-factor authentication.
  • b) Sharing your work email address freely on social media.
  • c) Verifying the identity of individuals requesting sensitive information.
  • d) Reporting suspicious emails or requests to the IT/security team.

5. How confident are you now in your ability to recognise and respond to social engineering attacks?

  • a) Not confident
  • b) Slightly confident
  • c) Moderately confident
  • d) Very confident

6. Scenario:

You receive an email from your company's HR department asking you to download an attachment with your updated salary information. The email looks legitimate, but you notice a few oddities in the sender's email address and the language used.

What steps should you take before opening the attachment?

7. Scenario:

During a busy workday, you receive a phone call from someone claiming to be from your IT department, asking you to provide your login credentials to resolve an urgent issue.

How should you handle this request?

8. Explain how multi-factor authentication (MFA) helps protect against social engineering attacks.

9. Describe a situation where you would report a suspicious email or activity to your IT/security team. What steps would you take in the reporting process?
