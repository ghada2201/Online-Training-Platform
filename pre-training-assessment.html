Pre-Training Assessment

1. Which of the following best describes social engineering?

  • a) A method used to break into computer systems using technical vulnerabilities.
  • b) A technique that involves manipulating people into divulging confidential information.
  • c) A way to enhance the efficiency of engineering processes.
  • d) None of the above.

2. Which of the following is a common characteristic of a phishing email?

  • a) The email asks you to click on a link to verify your account.
  • b) The email is sent from a colleague's official company email address.
  • c) The email is free from grammatical errors.
  • d) The email provides detailed information about the sender's background.

3. What should you do if you receive a suspicious email that appears to be from your bank?

  • a) Click the link in the email to verify your account details.
  • b) Ignore the email and delete it.
  • c) Report the email to your IT department or security team.
  • d) Reply to the email asking for more information.

4. Which of the following is an example of a social engineering attack?

  • a) Using a brute force attack to crack a password.
  • b) Creating a fake website to steal login credentials.
  • c) Sending an email with a malicious attachment.
  • d) Both b and c.

5. How likely are you to verify the identity of someone asking for sensitive information over the phone?

  • a) Very unlikely
  • b) Unlikely
  • c) Likely
  • d) Very likely

6. Scenario:

You receive an email from what appears to be your IT department, asking you to update your password immediately by clicking a link in the email.

What steps should you take to verify the authenticity of the email?

7. Scenario:

A person you don’t recognise tries to follow you into a secure area of the building, claiming they forgot their ID card.

What should you do in this situation?

8. In your own words, describe what social engineering is and why it is a threat to organisations.

9. List two common indicators that an email might be a phishing attempt.
