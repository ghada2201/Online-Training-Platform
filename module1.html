Module 1: Introduction to Social Engineering

(Foundational Module)

Training Objectives

  • Introduce the concept of social engineering and its relevance in the workplace.
  • Equip employees with the skills to recognise common social engineering tactics.
  • Provide practical examples to illustrate how social engineering attacks unfold.
  • Summarise key takeaways and practical tips for preventing social engineering attacks.

Key Points

  • Definition of social engineering.
  • Common types of social engineering attacks: phishing, pretexting, baiting, and tailgating.
  • Indicators of a social engineering attack (e.g., unusual requests, urgent language).
  • Phishing email characteristics: suspicious links, spoofed email addresses, urgent requests for information.
  • Detailed case studies of successful and unsuccessful social engineering attempts.
  • Recognising the Red Flags

This module will take approximately 1 hour to complete. Please ensure that you are prepared to fully engage with the content and participate in the interactive elements.
